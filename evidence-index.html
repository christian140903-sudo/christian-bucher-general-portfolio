Source coverage audit

The internal audit identified the main AI root as a very large source tree with more than one hundred thousand files, plus separate Miguel brain/vault folders, Miguel v2 idea notes, and a small verification stub. Public artifacts do not expose those raw folders. They extract only source clusters that are useful for reviewers: daemon/control-plane code, remote-agent workflow notes, routing/cascade files, memory systems, voice code, ANIMA tests, AMR text exports, FFG call pack materials, IP-style documents, and quantum red-team reports.

Some iCloud/PDF paths appeared as placeholder or cloud-backed files during the initial scan. Readable text exports and duplicate Drive copies were used where available. The public package therefore avoids pretending that every raw path was directly publishable; it uses evidence that was actually readable or documented in the control folder.

Why not publish the raw source folder?

The raw material contains private local paths, channel identifiers, credentials, personal notes, possible legal/IP material, and unfinished experiments. A raw dump would be less persuasive and less safe. The professional move is a curated evidence dossier: enough mechanism to prove depth, enough boundary to show judgment, and enough structure that a reviewer can understand the work quickly.
