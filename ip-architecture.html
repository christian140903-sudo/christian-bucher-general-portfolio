IP and PCT-Style Architecture

The AMR state-space work was also translated into formal IP-style documents: technical field, prior-art problem, system overview, dynamic graph/update mechanisms, prediction outputs, working examples, industrial applicability, and claim architecture.

What this demonstrates

  • Ability to convert an idea into technical/legal structure.
  • Separation of system, method, and computer-readable-medium claims.
  • Dependent-claim thinking around data types, uncertainty, stability, graph extension, CSI, Bayesian and machine-learning updates.
  • Understanding that patent/IP material must be handled carefully and not casually published in full if confidentiality matters.
Boundary: The public portfolio says "prepared IP-style documentation" unless official filing/grant evidence is separately verified.

Professional value

The value for the portfolio is not a legal conclusion. It is the ability to formalize a technical system precisely enough that it can be discussed in invention, implementation, and regulatory terms.

What the IP-style work shows

The professional value is formalization. A broad idea was translated into the structure expected of technical protection documents: field, prior art, problem, system summary, embodiments, working examples, advantages, industrial applicability, and claim layers. That is not a legal conclusion; it is evidence of precision under a demanding format.

The safest public presentation is therefore architectural. It can explain that system, method, and computer-readable-medium layers were drafted around dynamic probabilistic AMR state-space modeling, while keeping exact claim text and legal status out of the public package unless I decide to disclose it.

Why this matters generally

  • It shows capacity to move from concept to implementable system language.
  • It shows understanding of parameterization: resistance, uncertainty, stability, graph transitions, CSI, Bayesian/ML updates.
  • It shows the ability to think across invention, implementation, and regulatory communication.
  • It should remain secondary in the Fellowship package because Secure AI reviewers need agent-infrastructure evidence first.

Architecture layers explained

Data layer

Time-resolved microbiological, phenotypic, genotypic, literature, or experimental observations can be treated as inputs. Public wording stays abstract because clinical data handling would require governance and validation.

State layer

Resistance states are represented as graph nodes or state-space elements. Each state carries resistance, uncertainty, and stability rather than a single static label.

Transition layer

Edges describe possible movement between states: resistance gain or loss, collateral sensitivity, cross-resistance, or context-dependent transition probabilities.

Update layer

New observations update probabilities and parameters, with Bayesian or machine-learning update paths described in the technical drafting.

Prediction layer

The system can output trajectories, risk indicators, uncertainty intervals, stability changes, and intervention-relevant visualizations.

Interface layer

The public value is not a claim of medical advice. It is a structured way to present uncertainty-aware outputs for research or decision-support exploration.

Public disclosure discipline

The portfolio should not publish full legal claim text by default. A reviewer does not need raw claim wording to understand the skill. They need to see that the idea was formalized into a system architecture with implementable modules and claim boundaries. The exact legal texts can remain private or be provided selectively if I decide that disclosure is safe.

This also protects the tone. "I prepared formal IP-style architecture materials" is more credible than "I have a patent" unless official status is fully verified. The careful version is stronger because it is harder to attack.
